üõí What You Need

  • For Baby Journal: Baby journal book or blank notebook
  • Pens, colored pencils, or markers
  • Ultrasound photos and pregnancy photos
  • Glue stick or tape for mementos
  • Optional: Stickers, washi tape, decorative supplies
  • For Gender Reveal: Gender reveal supplies (balloons, confetti, cake, or scratch-off cards)
  • Camera or phone to capture the moment
  • Optional: Friends/family to share the moment (in-person or virtual)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start Your Journal: Choose any journal - pre-made or DIY. Write an opening letter about discovering your pregnancy.
  2. Document Milestones: Record first heartbeat, first kick, cravings, funny moments, fears, dreams. Add photos and ultrasound pictures.
  3. Write Letters to Baby: Share your hopes, fears, and what you're excited to teach them.
  4. Include Your Partner: Have them write entries with their feelings, predictions, and promises.
  5. Plan Gender Reveal: Choose intimate or large gathering. Pick a method that feels right for you.
  6. Simple Reveal Ideas: Envelope opening, balloon pop, cake cutting, box opening, scratch-off cards, or paint reveal.
  7. Capture the Moment: Record reactions - all emotions are beautiful and valid.
  8. Process Feelings: Any disappointment about gender is normal. Journal honestly and know feelings will evolve.

‚ú® Creative Add-On Ideas

  • Monthly Interview: Answer same questions monthly and watch answers evolve

  • Dream Journal: Record vivid pregnancy dreams in a separate section
  • Music Playlist: Note which songs make baby move or calm down
  • Time Capsule: Have guests write predictions to open on baby's first birthday
  • Virtual Reveal: Send scratch-offs to distant family for video call reveal
  • Sibling Involvement: Let older kids decorate pages and help with planning
  • Voice Messages: Record audio messages - baby can hear in 3rd trimester
