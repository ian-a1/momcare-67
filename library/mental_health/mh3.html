üõí What You Need

  • For Virtual Groups: Computer, tablet, or smartphone with internet
  • Zoom, Facebook, or other platform app (varies by group)
  • Headphones for privacy (optional but helpful)
  • For In-Person Groups: Transportation to meeting location
  • Comfortable clothing
  • Water bottle and light snacks
  • Open mind and willingness to share (at your comfort level)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Find Your Groups: Try prenatal yoga, hospital support groups, birthing classes, or fitness classes. Online: Facebook due date groups, BabyCenter, Reddit (r/BabyBumps), or Peanut app.
  2. Research First: Read descriptions and rules. Check if moderated. Browse posts for positive tone. Consider specific focus (high-risk, LGBTQ+, first-time moms).
  3. Start by Observing: It's okay to "lurk" first. Get familiar with group dynamics and topics before participating.
  4. Introduce Yourself: Share due date and journey when ready. Start small, share more as comfortable.
  5. Participate Regularly: Comment, ask questions, share wins and challenges. Others likely have same questions.
  6. Build Individual Connections: Message people you connect with. Exchange contacts. Meet in person if local (public places first).
  7. Attend Meetups: Join video calls or local gatherings for deeper connections and lasting friendships.
  8. Set Boundaries: Mute notifications if overwhelming. Take breaks. Leave unsupportive groups.

üë• Types of Communities to Consider

  • Due Date Groups: Same-month moms sharing milestones together
  • Location-Based: Local groups for in-person meetups
  • Special Circumstances: High-risk, loss, LGBTQ+, single/teen/older moms
  • Activity-Based: Yoga, swimming, walking - fitness plus socializing
  • Professional-Led: Hospital groups with nurse/counselor guidance
  • Cultural/Language: Connect in native language or shared culture

  • Book Club: Read pregnancy/parenting books together
  • Partner Events: Organize gatherings for partners to connect
