üìñ About This Practice

Slowly tense and release each muscle group from toes to head. Focus on tensing for 5 seconds, then relaxing for 30 seconds. This helps you recognize the difference between tension and relaxation, becoming more aware of physical sensations and stress.

  • Improves focus and mood

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Reminders

  • After 20 weeks, avoid lying flat - use reclined or left-side position
  • Tense gently (50-70% effort), not to point of pain
  • Skip tensing calves/feet if prone to leg cramps
  • Stop if dizzy, short of breath, or in pain
  • Consult provider if high-risk pregnancy

üõí What You Need

  • Comfortable surface - bed, yoga mat, or carpeted floor with cushions
  • Pregnancy pillow or regular pillows for support
  • Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing

  • Optional: Guided PMR audio recording or app

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Get Comfortable: Lie on left side or recline with back support. Use pillows for comfort. Close eyes, take 3 deep breaths.
  2. Feet: Curl toes, tense feet for 5 seconds. Release, relax 30 seconds. Notice warmth and heaviness.
  3. Calves: Point toes toward head, hold 5 seconds (skip if cramp-prone). Release 30 seconds.
  4. Thighs: Squeeze by pushing knees together, hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds.
  5. Buttocks/Hips: Squeeze together, hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds.
  6. Abdomen: Gently tighten (light engagement), hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds.
  7. Back: Arch slightly, squeeze shoulder blades, hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds.
  8. Hands/Arms: Make fists, tense forearms, hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds. Then bend arms, fists to shoulders, hold 5 seconds, release 30 seconds.
  9. Shoulders: Shrug up to ears, hold 5 seconds. Drop completely, relax 30 seconds.
  10. Neck: Gently tilt head back or press into pillow, hold 5 seconds. Release 30 seconds.
  11. Face: Scrunch all facial muscles, hold 5 seconds. Completely relax 30 seconds.
  12. Body Scan: Scan toes to head. Release any remaining tension. Take 3-5 deep breaths, rest 1-2 minutes.
  13. Return: Wiggle fingers/toes, breathe deeply, open eyes. Roll to side, use arms to sit up slowly.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Best times: Morning, before bed, during anxiety, or before appointments
  • Start with 5 minutes on most tense areas (shoulders, neck, jaw)
  • Use during labor between contractions
  • Breathe in while tensing, out while releasing
  • Track which areas hold most tension
  • Practice makes perfect - body responds faster with repetition
  • Have partner guide you - great for bonding and labor prep
  • Falling asleep during practice is okay - body needs rest!
