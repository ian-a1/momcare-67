üõí Ingredients

  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed

  • 1 piece chicken breast or thigh, cooked and shredded
  • 1 thumb-sized piece fresh ginger, sliced thinly
  • 2 teaspoons salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Optional toppings: hard-boiled egg, crispy garlic, calamansi

  1. Pour 5 cups of water (or low-sodium chicken broth) into a cooking pot. Add the thinly sliced ginger and bring to a boil over high heat. The ginger will infuse the broth with its soothing, aromatic flavor.
  2. Add 1 cup of rinsed long grain white rice to the boiling water. Reduce heat to medium-low. Continue cooking for 30 minutes or until the rice breaks down and the texture becomes thick and porridge-like. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  3. Add 2 teaspoons of salt (or adjust to your taste preference). Stir well to distribute the seasoning evenly. Cook for 2 more minutes.
  4. While the porridge is cooking, prepare the chicken topping: Cook the chicken breast or thigh thoroughly (165¬∞F internal temperature), then shred into small, bite-sized pieces.
  5. Transfer the hot lugaw to serving bowls. Top each bowl with a generous tablespoon of shredded chicken for protein.

  7. Serve hot as a comforting meal. This is especially soothing during morning sickness episodes. Share and enjoy!
