üõí Ingredients

  • 3 pieces medium chayote (sayote), peeled, seed removed, and sliced
  • 1/4 lb ground pork
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium sized onion, sliced
  • 1 medium sized tomato, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup water (optional, if needed)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Saut√© the crushed garlic until fragrant and golden, about 1 minute. Add the sliced onion and chopped tomato. Continue saut√©ing until the onion becomes translucent and the tomato softens, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add 1/4 lb of ground pork to the pan. Break up the pork with a spatula and cook for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pork is fully browned and cooked through with no pink remaining.
  4. Add the sliced chayote to the pan. Stir well to combine with the pork and aromatics, ensuring all pieces are coated.
  5. Cover the pan with a lid. Let the mixture come to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 7 to 10 minutes until the chayote becomes tender but still has a slight crunch. Add 1/4 cup water if the mixture becomes too dry.
  6. Remove the lid. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper. Stir well to distribute the seasonings evenly. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  7. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot with steamed rice as a side dish or light lunch. Share and enjoy this nutritious postpartum meal!
