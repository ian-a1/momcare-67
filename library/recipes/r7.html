üìñ About This Recipe

A beloved Bicolano dish featuring taro leaves (gabi) cooked in rich coconut milk and cream. This nutrient-dense recipe is especially beneficial for breastfeeding mothers, providing essential vitamins A and C, calcium, and iron. The creamy coconut milk offers healthy fats that support baby's brain development through breast milk. This flavorful, spicy dish is traditionally enjoyed as a side with steamed rice.

  • Rich in vitamin A, vitamin C, calcium, and iron essential for breastfeeding mothers
  • Iron helps produce more blood to supply oxygen and nutrients to you and baby
  • Coconut milk provides healthy fats for baby's brain development through breast milk

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Reminders

  • Cook taro leaves thoroughly (minimum 45 minutes) to break down calcium oxalate crystals that can cause itching
  • Use fresh coconut milk or quality canned versions (check expiration dates)
  • Start with small portions to test tolerance, especially if you're breastfeeding
  • Adjust spice level (red chilies) based on your tolerance and baby's reaction through breast milk

üõí Ingredients

  • 3.5 oz dried taro leaves (gabi leaves)
  • 6 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • 1/2 lb pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup shrimp paste (bagoong or balaw)
  • 5 to 7 pieces red chilies (siling labuyo) - adjust to taste
  • 1 piece onion, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sliced ginger
  • 8 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Salt and pepper to taste (optional)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Cooking Instructions

  1. Combine 6 cups of coconut milk, thinly sliced pork, 1/2 cup shrimp paste, sliced ginger, sliced onion, and crushed garlic in a large cooking pot. Heat the pot over medium-high heat and let the mixture come to a boil.
  2. Once the mixture starts to boil, gently stir to mix all the ingredients thoroughly. Cover the pot with a lid and reduce heat to low. Simmer for 15 to 20 minutes until the pork becomes tender. Stir occasionally to prevent the ingredients from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  3. Add 3.5 oz of dried taro leaves to the pot. Important: Do not stir immediately. Let the leaves sit on top and slowly absorb the coconut milk. This takes about 20 to 30 minutes. You can gently push the leaves down into the liquid so they can absorb more coconut milk, but avoid stirring vigorously.
  4. Once the taro leaves have absorbed most of the coconut milk and become soft, gently stir the leaves to mix them with the other ingredients. Continue to cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Pour 2 cups of coconut cream into the cooking pot. Add 5 to 7 pieces of red chilies (adjust based on your spice tolerance). Stir gently to combine. Cook for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. The shrimp paste already provides saltiness, so add additional salt only if necessary.
  7. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot with steamed rice as a flavorful side dish. Share and enjoy this nutritious Bicolano delicacy!
