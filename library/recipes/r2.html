üõí Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs. salmon fillet (or bangus/milkfish, or tilapia)
  • 3 10g pouches Knorr Sinigang na Isda mix
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach), leaves removed and stalks cut into small pieces
  • 10 pieces snake beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch length
  • 2 medium ripe tomatoes, sliced into wedges
  • 6 to 8 pieces okra
  • 6 ounces radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 medium yellow onion, wedged
  • 4 pieces long green peppers (siling haba)
  • 3 tablespoons patis (fish sauce)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 1/2 quarts water

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Cooking Instructions

  1. Pour 1 1/2 quarts of water into a cooking pot. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add tomato wedges, onion, and daikon radish slices to the boiling water. Cover the pot and cook for 5 minutes to soften the vegetables and release their flavors.
  3. Add the salmon fillet to the pot. Cook for 2 minutes without stirring to prevent the fish from breaking apart.
  4. Add the 3 pouches of Knorr Sinigang na Isda mix. Stir gently to dissolve the seasoning completely in the broth.
  5. Add the long green peppers, okra, and snake beans to the pot. Cover and cook over medium heat for 2 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Add the kangkong stalks first. Season with 3 tablespoons patis and 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes until stalks are tender.
  7. Add the kangkong leaves last. Turn off the heat immediately. Cover the pot and let it sit for 5 minutes, allowing the residual heat to wilt the leaves while keeping their bright green color and nutrients intact.
  8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve hot with steamed rice. Share and enjoy!
