Staying active enhances energy, reduces discomforts like back pain and fatigue, and supports smoother delivery and recovery. Exercise during pregnancy has minimal risks for uncomplicated cases, promoting fitness, preventing gestational diabetes, managing weight, and improving well-being.[1] Studies show aerobic exercise improves fitness, while combined diet/exercise reduces excessive weight gain and gestational diabetes risk.[2] Postnatally, it also aids recovery, strengthens muscles, and prevents incontinence.[3] Consult your doctor first, especially with contraindications like heart disease. Aim for 150 minutes weekly.[4]

üí™ Exercise Overview

1. Gentle Walking
Prenatal: 10-30 mins. daily
Benefits: Boosts energy, eases back pain, improves mood[1]
Precautions: Avoid uneven ground
Postnatal: 10-20 mins; starting week 1
Benefits: Aids recovery, reduces swelling[2]
2. Seated Stretches
Prenatal: 5-10 mins.
Benefits: Relieves tension, promotes relaxation[1]
Precautions: Sit tall
Postnatal: 5 mins. daily
Benefits: Eases aches, restores posture[5]
3. Breathing Exercises
Prenatal: 5-10 min. deep breaths
Benefits: Reduces stress, prepares for labor[6]
Postnatal: 5 mins. daily
Benefits: Reduces anxiety, improves sleep[7]
4. Pelvic Floor Exercises (Kegels)
Prenatal: 10-20 repetitions, 3 times daily
Benefits: Strengthens for labor, prevents incontinence[3]
Notes: Squeeze 5-10 sec. per repetition
Postnatal: 10-50 reps daily
Benefits: Treats incontinence, supports postnatal recovery[3]
Notes: Squeeze 5-10 sec. per repetition
5. Maternity Yoga
Prenatal: 5-15 min. poses
Benefits: Increases flexibility, reduces stress, eases back pain[8]
Precautions: Avoid back-lying poses after trimester 1
Postnatal: 10 min. gentle flows
Benefits: Improves mood, strengthens core[1]
6. Pilates
Prenatal: 10-15 mins; core-focused
Benefits: Improves posture and strength, aids flexibility, mitigates GDM when done along with diet[9]
Notes: Modify for belly
Postnatal: 10 min. mat work
Benefits: Restores abdominal strength[1]
7. Supported Squats
Prenatal: 5-10 repetitions with support
Benefits: Opens pelvis, builds legs, eases labor pain[8]
Precautions: Avoid deep repetitions if unstable
Postnatal: 5 repetitions, post-week 6
Benefits: Strengthens lower body for daily tasks[8]
8. Stationary Cycling
Prenatal: 10-20 mins.
Benefits: Cardio without joint strain, low-impact, high-quality aerobic exercise for fitness during pregnancy[2]
Precautions: Adjust for balance
Postnatal: 10 mins; starting week 2-4
Benefits: Rebuilds endurance[2]
Precautions: Gradual increase per week
9. Light Aerobics
Prenatal: 10-15 mins; low-impact
Benefits: Boosts mood and coordination, prevents weight gain[2]
Postnatal: 10 mins; gradual
Benefits: Enhances energy, family bonding[10]
10. Swimming/Water Aerobics
Prenatal: 15-20 mins; low-impact
Benefits: Relieves joint pressure, reduces back/pelvic pain, aids buoyancy[11]
Postnatal: 10-15 mins; post-clearance
Benefits: Boosts circulation, gentle exercise for healing[8]
