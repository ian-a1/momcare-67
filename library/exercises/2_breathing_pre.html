üí® Breathing Exercise Examples

Diaphragmatic (Belly) Breathing
  1. Inhale deeply through the nose for 4 counts, expanding the belly while keeping shoulders relaxed
  2. Exhale slowly through the mouth for 4-6 counts
Benefits:[12]
Helps manage shortness of breath
Coordinates diaphragm with pelvic floor for labor preparation[13]
Patterned or Slow Breathing
  1. Inhale through the nose
  2. Exhale through the mouth in a rhythmic pattern (e.g., 4-7-8 second breathing: inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8)
Benefits:[17]
Reduces labor pain and anxiety
Supports relaxation techniques for pain management[6]
Prenatal Yoga Breathing (Ujjayi Breath)
  1. Practice gentle throat-constricted breathing during yoga poses
  2. Inhale and exhale evenly through the nose
Benefits:[12]
Promotes mental centering and eases contractions
Organizing Breath
  1. Take a deep inhale at the start of a contraction, followed by slow exhales
Benefits:[15]
Prepares for labor by focusing energy and reducing preterm labor risks through relaxation
