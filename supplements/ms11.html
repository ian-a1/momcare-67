Medicines for Allergies

Cetirizine & Budesonide

üìñ About These Medicines

  • Cetirizine: An antihistamine used to relieve allergy symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, and hives. Considered safe in pregnancy, especially after the first trimester.
  • Budesonide: A corticosteroid that reduces airway inflammation. Safest inhaled steroid option in pregnancy for managing asthma or allergic rhinitis. Helps keep breathing under control and prevents asthma attacks.

üìã Dosage & Use

  • Cetirizine Dosage: 10 mg once daily (standard tablet or syrup).
  • Cetirizine When to take: Once daily, preferably in the evening or when allergy symptoms appear.
  • Budesonide Dosage: Inhaler: 200-400 mcg twice daily (depending on doctor's advice). Nasal spray: 1 spray per nostril daily.
  • Budesonide When to take: Daily, at the same time each day for asthma/allergy control. Do not skip doses if prescribed for maintenance.
