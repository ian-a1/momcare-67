MomCare Guide – Frequently Asked Questions

What is MomCare Guide?
MomCare Guide is a free Progressive Web App (PWA) offering verified, culturally relevant pregnancy support for Filipino women within Sta. Mesa, Manila. It includes a pregnancy milestone tracking, maternal health library, emergency SOS, and community forum — all accessible and installable from any browser and device type.
Is it free and safe to use?
Yes, the app is completely free with no hidden costs. It follows RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act), uses encryption for all personal data, and never sells user information. Only verified healthcare content is allowed.
What devices can run it?
Primarily any mobile or desktop device with a modern browser (Chrome, Safari, etc.) and at least 10 MB of free space. It runs as a Progressive Web App (PWA) — no app store download needed — and works on low-end devices with at least 2 GB of RAM or more.
How do I sign up or log in?
  • Open MomCare Guide
  • Select Sign-Up
  • Enter your: Full name, Email, Birthdate, Gender, Address, and Password.
  • To log in, use your email and password.
  • Forgot password? Use the reset link sent to your email.
Can I use it without internet?
  • Yes — install MomCare Guide to be able to use it without internet.
  • Pregnancy tracking and local data will work without internet (except for Emergency SOS);
  • Forum and any updates will sync once you’re back online.
How do I access health information?
Go to the Library section to browse verified maternal health resources inside the app. All content is medically reviewed and culturally tailored for Filipino use.
How does pregnancy tracking work?
  • Go to Profile tab.
  • Select Basic Information.
  • Find the Estimated Due Date field.
  • Enter your due date to see your current pregnancy progress;
  • Additionally, you can select Don't know your due date? Learn more option below to get more information regarding your Estimated Due Date.
  • Add prenatal appointments with dates and reminders based on your current pregnancy plan.
  • The app sends alerts so you never miss a check-up — all saved locally and synced when online.
What is the community forum?
MomCare Guide Community Forum is a moderated space where users can ask questions, share experiences, and support each other. All posts are reviewed by admins to prevent misinformation and misuse. Report any concerns directly in-app.
How does the Emergency SOS work?
  • Tap the Emergency SOS button to figure out your current location.
  • MomCare Guide will send a pre-set message regarding your information to your emergency contacts and clinic.
  • Mobile data or Internet access is required to be able to use this feature.
Can my partner or family use it?
Yes — Partners can also create accounts to access educational content, community forum, and other relevant features in-app. MomCare Guide app encourages active family involvement for ensuring maternal health support.
How is my data protected and backed up?
Personal data is encrypted and stored securely in the cloud with daily backups. Local data (like library) is cached on your device. Regular syncs ensure your information is up-to-date when online.
Who checks the medical accuracy?
All library content is reviewed and approved by licensed healthcare professionals and aligned with Department of Health (DOH) guidelines in relation to maternal health. Admin moderators ensure ongoing accuracy and cultural relevance.
What if I have technical issues?
  • Try refreshing the page or clearing cache.
  • For login or sync problems, check your internet and retry.
  • For other issues, contact support via in-app Help & Support section.
How is the app different from BabyCenter or What to Expect?
Unlike global apps, MomCare Guide is built specifically for Filipino use, with PWA architecture (MomCare Guide size <= 10 MB; global apps <= 500 MB), with unique features like Emergency SOS tied to Philippine protocols.
How will the app be improved after launch?
User feedback via surveys and Likert-scale evaluations (per ISO 25010) will guide updates. Future versions may add voice mode, chatbot support, or integration with other public health centers.
